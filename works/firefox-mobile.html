Firefox Lite

background

Firefox Rocket(now Firefox Lite) is a lightweight Android browser customized for emerging markets. It reached 200K downloads with a rating of 4.5 within 2 months, and reached 1M downloads one year after launch in Indonesia.

I was one of the two main UX designers leading the project, participated from early concept ideation, field research, prototyping, user testing to the official app launch.

Research

Indonesia has the 4th largest population and 132 million of internet user. It is one of the biggest emerging market in the world with huge opportunity of growth. To understand the market and users, the UX team traveled to Indonesia for field trip three times in 6 months. We observed how phone shaped people’s daily lives by talking to numerous users, watching how people use apps they use everyday, and immersing ourselves in the culture.

Insights

From several rounds of user research, the team identified user needs and insights. We also ran multiple quantitative surveys to prioritize and verify our findings.

Some key insights are:

  • Speed is the key to web browsing: Due to slow and unstable internet connection, web browsing experience is poor.
  • App size matters: Internal storage space is limited and internet connection is unstable.
  • Data and price sensitive: Low loyalty towards any mobile phone brands, carrier, or apps.
  • Word of mouth marketing: Multiple methods to acquire apps and knowing an app mostly from word of mouth.

Ideation

Based on research findings and market insights, the team hosted several brainstorming sessions. We conducted another round of user testing with prototype to validate the design and concepts. In order to meet the tight development timeline for initial launch, we were very focused on the most basic and a few differentiation features.
